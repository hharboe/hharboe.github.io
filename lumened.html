LUMENED: CONNECTING CLASSROOMS

LumenEd was the education technology startup I co-founded my junior year at Oberlin College, and worked on full-time after graduation. We shipped a hardware, software, and curriculum product package to K-12 classrooms. For every package sold, we donated the equivalent to an underprivileged classroom in India.

MY ROLE

I was one of three co-founders, and the design, sales, and marketing lead. In addition to plenty of grunt and strategic work as co-founder, my responsibilities included launching our curriculum and software product, leading our sales initiative with K-12 schools, and designing all our web, print, and video materials.

My two co-founders, Prakash Paudel, and Thomas Kreek, lead development/fundraising, and hardware development, respectively.

OVERVIEW

THREE YEARS AND COUNTING...

LumenEd’s traction started on stage in Ohio, at a pitch competition, where three college juniors held a cardboard box holding an OEM projector and Raspberry Pi. Three years later, we sat in our apartment/office in Chicago, adding custom wax seals to 150 sales letters as a last-ditch effort to reach our revenue targets. We had four employees working in New Delhi, thirty of our custom projectors deployed to classrooms around India, and absolutely $0 in our bank accounts.

Our products included “The Bright Orange Box” (BOB), a battery-powered smart projector designed for offline classrooms, and “Video Pen Pals,” a classroom-to-classroom relationship for K-8 students. At the time we ended operations in summer 2016, we had deployed thirty units of BOB, and were ready to start our third consecutive year of Video Pen Pals.

Along the way, some highlights included:

  • Selling $45k in classroom subscriptions in our first three months of sales
  • Raising $100k in grants, Kickstarter, and pre-seed investment
  • Building a 150+ customer pipeline of American schools
  • Hiring a team of 5 education superstars in our New Delhi office
  • Deploying 30 custom projectors to Indian classrooms (many of which are still in use)
  • Being a Keynote speaker at the 2015 Global Education Conference
