MICHAEL: CYCLING COMPANION

In October 2017, a friend contracted me to prototype and pitch a chatbot to his employer, an electric bike startup in San Francisco. This is the story of how I helped create a software companion to urban cyclists.

MY ROLE

I was responsible for the experience strategy of the chatbot, from preliminary research through beta testing. I also designed the character, conversation scheme, and all visual components within the chatbot interface.

Development in Microsoft’s bot framework was lead by Prakash Paudel with help from Thomas Kreek.

BACKSTORY

OF BICYCLES AND CHATBOTS

This project was initiated by my past collaborator, Thomas Kreek, who for years has been frustrated by the lack of commuter cycling in the US.

In fall 2017, he started developing a chatbot for his employer, electric bike startup, Faraday Bicycles. He hoped a chatbot would improve customer engagement, and bring more attention to Faraday’s brand in the cycling and tech communities.

Faraday contracted me to build a prototype around this concept. With their brand styleguides in hand, I went to work creating a character and iOS interface around the “cycling companion” concept Thomas proposed. I used the likeness of Michael Faraday, famous 19th century physicist (and namesake of the company), as the chatbot character providing tools to cyclists.
